Investigation Doctrine

Methodology for tracing fraudsters through lawful process
Golden Rule: You do not hack the suspect. Evidence is built through legal notices to telecoms & banks (§94 BNSS), CDR/IPDR, and inter-agency portals. The case closes on the criminal's mistakes, not their disguises.

Fraudsters fake the phone, the Aadhaar, and the bank account — so investigators ignore those and pursue the four things a criminal cannot fake, always through lawful process.

1. The Money Trail

Stolen money must exit somewhere. Follow it through mule accounts to the cash-out — ATM CCTV, the final wallet's KYC, the IP that authorised the last transfer. The exit point is the weak spot.

2. The Device — IMEI

A fake SIM inside a real phone still exposes the handset's IMEI. One IMEI seen across many fraud SIMs links them to a single device. CDR/IPDR reveal IMEI, cell towers, and login IPs.

3. Reused Patterns

Same recovery email, a UPI handle reused on an old classified ad, the same withdrawal ATM, the same script and active hours. Correlating many fake identities to one operator — exactly what SCINT models.

4. Point of Sale

Every fake SIM was activated by a real retailer using forged documents. The activation agent, location, and bulk pattern are traceable — it's how SIM-fraud rings get cracked.
